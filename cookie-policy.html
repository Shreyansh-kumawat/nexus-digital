What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and to provide useful information to website owners.

Cookies do not contain any personally identifiable information on their own — they typically store a unique identifier that allows the website to recognise your browser on repeat visits.

Think of cookies like a name tag: They help the website remember who you are so it doesn’t treat you like a stranger every time you visit.

Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies on nexusdigital.io to:

  • Ensure the website functions correctly (essential operations)
  • Remember your preferences across sessions (e.g., theme, language)
  • Analyse how visitors use our website to improve the experience
  • Measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns
  • Deliver relevant content and ads based on your interests (with your consent)
  • Prevent fraud and ensure the security of our platform

Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our site are set by third-party services we use. These include:

  • Google Analytics — Website traffic analysis (Privacy Policy)
  • Google Ads — Conversion tracking and remarketing
  • Meta Pixel — Facebook & Instagram ad performance tracking
  • LinkedIn Insight Tag — LinkedIn campaign attribution
  • Hotjar — Heatmaps and session recordings (anonymised)
  • Intercom / Crisp — Live chat functionality

These third parties have their own privacy and cookie policies. We recommend reviewing them if you have concerns about how they handle your data.

Cookie Duration

Cookies on our website fall into two duration categories:

  • Session cookies — Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. Used for essential site functions like form submissions and page navigation.
  • Persistent cookies — Remain on your device for a set period (typically 30 days to 2 years) or until you delete them. Used for preferences, analytics, and marketing.
Most of our analytics cookies expire within 14 months. Marketing cookies from Google and Meta typically persist for up to 90 days.

Managing Cookies

You have full control over which cookies you accept. You can manage your preferences in the following ways:

  • Cookie consent banner — When you first visit our site, a banner will ask for your consent for non-essential cookies. You can accept all, reject optional ones, or customise your choices.
  • Update preferences anytime — Click the cookie icon in the bottom-left corner of our site to revisit your preferences.
  • Browser settings — You can block or delete cookies directly through your browser (see the table below).
  • Opt-out tools — Use Google Analytics Opt-out or YourOnlineChoices for ad cookies.
Note: Disabling essential cookies may break core functionality of the site (e.g., contact forms, navigation).

Your Consent

When you first visit nexusdigital.io, we will ask for your consent to place non-essential cookies (analytics, marketing, and social media cookies).

  • Clicking “Accept All” enables all cookies including optional ones
  • Clicking “Reject Optional” allows only essential and security cookies
  • Clicking “Customise” lets you choose which categories to enable

Your consent is stored for 12 months, after which we will ask for it again. You can change your preferences at any time via the cookie settings icon.

We never pre-tick optional cookies or use dark patterns to trick you into accepting more than you’re comfortable with.
